![2026-06-07](/assets/2026-06-07-week.svg)

Quieter than the week before. The changes went deeper instead of wider: real databases replacing placeholders, auth flows finished end to end, and courses with actual structure.

## sunday

bcgd flipped from a static site to a real operations platform, Supabase now backing the inventory and order data. That brings it from a landing page to something usable. Grapher picked up visible zoom controls across web, iOS, and macOS. Charters got the App Store mobile scroll fix. Beep hit bot detection on the transit site and reverted to the iframe approach for now.

## monday

Tally got smarter about monthly reporting. It now reads whether you already submitted a report from BC Self-Serve instead of guessing, and the manual toggle was tightened so it stops re-adding what was already removed. Brief got an amendment to the first case, adding the ICBC claim, mother's assistance during the incident, and the high-function PTSD dimension.

## wednesday

Dose got a password reset flow on web. Both sides: the forgot-password request and the recovery page that handles the link from the email. It was missing before.

## thursday

The iOS side of Dose caught up. Sign-up, forgot password, and password reset through the deep link all connect now. Auth wired into the sync flow and the settings screen. Tally removed a fake debt triage section and replaced the reporting banner with an Already Filed button.

## weekend

Lingo shipped its first real academic course. A full calculus sequence replaced the flat question bank, structured with a proper skill tree. School linked the Pre-Calc cram sheets for modules two through four directly from the dashboard.

Reconnected with Jayda for the first time in about ten years. Three days together, a couple of drives out to Vancouver to catch the sunset. Fun, but also exhausting in a way that's hard to explain. The whole stretch was one long memory lane, and what it mostly reminded me of is how much I value my own space, energy, and peace now.

The bigger move was submitting the application to Capilano University for Paralegal Studies. That's the next concrete step toward practicing law.
